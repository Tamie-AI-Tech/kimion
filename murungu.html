Keeping upright with Christ

BY David Stephens, BULAWAYO, ZIMBABWE. October 2023
Comments 26K Views
Introduction

It is widely publicized that keeping upright with our Lord Jesus Christ is life–bearing. But, how do we keep up with Him? This writing points us to the law of Christ, and shows us why it matters. While Paul said that we are “no longer under the law”, he meant that God’s people are no longer justified by the law. However, it’s important for us to note that we should not take God’s grace for granted. Thus, we should still obey the will and law of Jesus Christ. First, what is our status? We are sinners by nature. This nature separates us from God because He is holy (Isa. 59:2). We were created holy, but since our predecessors sinned against God from the Garden of Eden, we all by default became wilful sinners, children of wrath (Eph. 2:1-3; Rom. 3:23).

The bible is clear that the result of our sin and disobedience to God is the reason for the evil, tragedy and suffering in the world. It also declares that the consequence of our sin for each of us is physical and spiritual death (Rom. 6:23). Second, what is God’s Solution? Jesus, the Lamb of God was sent to take away our sins. He lived a perfect, sinless life and willingly died the death we deserved on the cross. In His death, Jesus both pacified the righteous anger of God, put away our sin and clothed us in His righteousness (Rom. 3:21-26; Heb. 2:17; 1 Jn. 2: 1-2 & 4: 8-10). The apostle Paul tells us that fulfilling scripture, Christ rose three days later, removing the penalty and destroying the power of sin (1 Cor. 15: 3-4). Now, with this gracious provision comes a wonderful gift. Third, what is God’s promise? The assurance given to every person who calls upon the name of the Lord is that when call unto Him, they will be saved (Rom. 10:13). Jesus and His apostles preached repentance of sins and belief on Him by faith (Mk. 1:5; Acts 3:26 & 8:22), whom God sent (Jn. 16:28). If we would do these things, then we shall be saved from Gods judgement (Jn. 3:16; Rom. 3:22 & 10:9). I would like to believe that this is not the first time that you have encountered the gospel. If it is, then I pray that the Lord may minister life to you. Building on the foundation of the gospel message, it is important to know who you are and what purpose God has for you. In other words, you have a new identity and destiny. It is a privilege and responsibility which each of us bears. We must, as Paul encourages us, now run our race with perseverance holding on to the hope that we have in Christ (Heb. 12:1). It is the Law of Christ which gives us hope. This Law outlines the boundaries within which we run our race. It is what provides for us our identity and destiny. The Law of Christ is to “love the Lord our God with all our heart, soul and strength and to love our neighbour as our self” (Lk. 10:27; Mt. 22:36-40; Mk. 12:28-31).

This is the moral law of Moses or the Ten Commandments restated by Christ. In Christ the ceremonial laws were fulfilled. Him being both the High Priest and the offering/sacrifice, which was acceptable to God on our behalf (Heb. 4:14-16; 1 Jn. 2:2). Jesus restated our responsibility to uphold the moral law in a more personal fashion in Mt. 5-7. In these chapters relationships are re-conceptualised by Christ, the way they were intended to be practiced. From negative to positive interpersonal and personal introspective ones. For instance, you shall not murder to whoever is angry with a brother. Also, you shall not commit adultery to if you look with lustful intent. Or, whoever would divorce for no reason should now only do so on grounds of adultery. Finally, we shall consider when people retaliated, they would do so equally – an eye for an eye. Jesus not only espoused but commands us to turn the other cheek and pray for our enemies. These two commands of Christ as we know, are what all the law and prophets hang upon.

Identity – The Heart of the Matter The first command, to love the Lord our God with all our heart, soul and strength is what provides us with our identity. It helps us to rightly relate to God. For so long, we have been self-centred and worshiping idols, that our hearts need an adjustment and this command is what does that. Understanding that the covenant we are in is not purely transactional. Where we do, give or say things expecting that God will give back to us more than we have given. Our relationship is to involve true commitment, regardless of whether we have abundance or not. We become God–centred, putting away from us our idols (whatever they may be). Realising that we exist to honour, worship and serve Him, not the other way around. He is our Father and will discipline, correct, guide and direct us as He sees fit. We must have faith, that He knows what is best, even if it is not what we want or is painful at the time and we do not understand our situation. Through it all we keep our eyes firmly fixed on the author and perfector of our faith (Heb. 12:2).

Conclusion

Destiny – A Matter of the Heart The second command provides us with our destiny or purpose. It is what helps us to rightly relate to God’s community.Honouring, caring for, counselling, teaching, reaching out to, worshipping and fellowshipping with your new family refocuses our heart. The Holy Spirit begins to work to sanctify and mature us in Christ. Helping us to love our neighbours, as we discover what our gift and passion is. This is why honouring Godly leaders is essential, as they help us to grow in our personal and ministerial walk with the Lord.
